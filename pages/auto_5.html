How to Compare Pre-Owned Cars

Looking for a used car to buy? Start by doing a little bit of research online. That simple step helps save a lot of time, effort, and possibly, even heartbreak later. If you’ve got a particular model in mind that fits your needs, start by comparing it with other cars in the same segment and price bracket. It will give you a good idea about where your target car stands. Consider looking for a model that has as many features as possible. This enhances its value and ensures that you get a good price when you decide to sell it. It’ll also be easier to sell. Check auto magazines, newspaper classifieds, and online listings to compare a few cars. You can also check the cars dealerships have online or ask them to email their listing to you. Visit several websites and use their tools and filters to compare cars as well. 

Tips for Smart Comparison

Mileage: Try and get a car with the lowest amount of mileage possible. A car with low mileage may cost more and be in better condition, but that’s not necessary and depends on how its previous owner maintained and used it. But, ideally, compare cars and look for one with low mileage. 

Warranty: Compare warranties on different vehicles to see what works best for you. This is because there are different types of warranties. There’s a popular but limited Merchantability Warranty. Then there’s the Manufacturer’s Warranty when the car is still under warranty from the manufacturer. Dealerships often have an “As Is” Warranty which means that the car is sold in the condition it is in currently without any warranty. There’s also an Extended Warranty that is usually offered on Certified Pre-Owned Cars. Get a copy of the warranty and understand what it covers. 

Mechanic: When you narrow your list to two or three cars, hire a trusted mechanic to evaluate them and give you a written report for each. Pay for it if needed. You’ll immediately know whether you should buy the car or not. 

Side-By-Side Comparison: Run side-by-side comparisons on the cars you like using online tools. Check things like engine capacity, transmission, infotainment and safety features, and more to finalize your choice.

Three Ways to Keep Your New Phone Protected

Follow these tips to keep your smartphone protected at all times

Use a Keylock or Pin Code

Did you know that about 54% of the smartphone users in the US do not use a password for the phones? If a phone is left unattended, lost, or stolen, anyone who picks the phone will get unrestricted access to all the data on the phone. This could lead to data being stolen, registering for unwanted services, and phone calls being made.

Protect your smartphone using a pattern lock, your fingerprint, or a PIN code. You can also set up a SIM card lock to prevent your SIM card from being stolen and used on another phone.

Protect Sensitive Data

Nowadays, preventing someone from accessing your phone isn’t enough. It’s very easy to plug your phone into a computer and access its data or remove the microSD card to retrieve data. Therefore, it is a must to protect sensitive data that might be saved to your internal storage. Many smartphone platforms feature software that can encrypt folders or files with industry-standard protection. This means that before a file is viewed or copied, a code must be entered. This also goes for information like account number, passwords, login details, and any other important information. This is to ensure that this information is not easily accessible to anyone. Some security vendors also recommend storing sensitive information on secure online servers remotely, instead of on the phone.

Backup Your Data

If you lose your phone, important information like contacts, messages, documents, and appointments might take a long time to replace. Therefore, you must take regular backups of your data.

Most smartphones allow users to ‘synchronize’ information with a website or computer for backup purposes. Some also allow an automatic backup facility to reduce the hassle of doing this manually.

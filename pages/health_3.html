Here’s Everything You Need To Know About Hearing Aids

While you might have heard a lot about hearing aids, you might be a little worried about whether it’ll really help and how it’ll look. Keep in mind that hearing aids can’t restore normal hearing. However, they can amplify the sounds that you have trouble hearing, and thus help improve your hearing. 

Different Hearing Aid Styles

Hearing aids differ in terms of special features, price, the way they’re placed in your ear, and size. 

Here are some of the common styles of hearing aids:

Completely in The Canal (CIC) or Mini CIC

These hearing aids are molded to fit inside your ear canal, and they help improve mild to moderate hearing loss in adults. 

– Least visible type of hearing aid

– Less likely to pick up wind noise

– Is susceptible to earwax clogging the speaker

– Might not include volume control and other such extra features

– Uses smaller batteries

– Has a shorter life

In the Canal (ITC)

An ITC device partly fits in the ear canal and is custom molded. It can help improve mild to moderate hearing loss in adults. 

– Less visible than larger styles

– Is susceptible to earwax clogging the speaker

– Includes features that are absent in CIC aids, but might be difficult to use because of its size

In the Ear (ITE)

This hearing aid is custom made in two different styles- one that fills only the lower part of your outer ear (half shell) and one that fills most of the bowl-shaped area of your outer ear (full shell). Both these styles are available with directional microphones and help people with mild to severe loss of hearing. 

– Includes volume control and other such features

– Uses a larger battery which gives it a longer life

– Might be easier to handle

– Is susceptible to earwax clogging the speaker

– More visible in the ear than smaller devices

– Might pick up more wind noise when compared to smaller devices

Behind the Ear (BTE)

This hearing aid rests behind the ear and hooks over the top of your ear. A tube connects this device to a ear mold (a custom earpiece), which fits in your ear canal. This type of hearing aid is useful for all age groups and can help improve almost any hearing loss.

– Had directional microphones

– Might pick up more wind noise than other styles

– Traditionally the largest type of device

– Capable of more amplification than other styles

Receiver in The Ear (RITE) Or Receiver in Canal (RIC)

The RITE and RIC styles are like the BTE devices, where the receiver of the speaker sits in the ear canal. Instead of a tube, a tiny wire connects the piece behind the ear to the receiver or speaker. 

– Has manual control options & directions microphones

– Has a less visible portion behind the ear

– Is susceptible to earwax clogging the speaker

Open Fit

This device is a variation of the BTE hearing aid. It uses a thin tube or the TIC or RITE hearing aid with an open dome in the ear. This device keeps the ear canal very open. This allows low-frequency sounds to naturally enter the ear. It also amplifies the high-frequency sounds. This is a good choice for those who experience high-frequency hearing loss.

– Might be difficult to insert into the ear because of the non-custom dome

– Is often visible

– Doesn’t plug the ear, which might make your own voice sound better to you
